Research Position

"Roofing near me" and "roofer near me" are generic local service searches. This study does not claim ownership of the words. It documents a safer and stronger strategy: publish the clearest machine-readable public record around the search intent.

The objective is evidence, not hype: consistent entity facts, real service-area proof, structured data, research citations, useful software, and honest homeowner guidance.

Not an Advertisement

This page is structured as research infrastructure. It classifies search intent, documents methods, maps software tools to homeowner workflows, and publishes machine-readable proof. Commercial pages may cite it, but the study itself is technical, non-promissory, and evidence-focused.

The Thesis

Modern local roofing visibility is becoming an entity-resolution problem. Search engines and AI answer systems must determine which contractor is local, legitimate, well-documented, useful, and safe to recommend for a given homeowner intent.

Entity Proof

Address, phone, service area, founder identity, profiles, citations, and sameAs links.

Research Proof

GitHub, Hugging Face, Kaggle, OSF, Zenodo, ORCID, Amazon Author, and press references.

Software Proof

Visibility mining, link architecture, rough measurement, secure scope, estimate tools, and evidence scorecards.

Own the Language

The strategy is not to claim legal ownership over generic phrases. The strategy is to make Inspector Roofing's preferred vocabulary the clearest, most consistent, most cited, and most machine-readable language layer around local roofing documentation.

The scientific method is simple: define the term, map it to intent, connect it to a territory or service, attach it to proof, express it with schema, preserve it with a DOI, and reuse it consistently across public surfaces.

The study includes a keyword ontology map that codes each phrase by head keyword, modifier, geo layer, intent class, owned-language bridge, proof artifact, and schema node.

Territory Coverage

The research maps money keywords and long-tail searches across Alpharetta, Roswell, Milton, Johns Creek, Cumming, Sandy Springs, Atlanta, Marietta, Canton, Duluth, Suwanee, Brookhaven, North Atlanta, and Metro Atlanta. Atlanta is handled as an honest service-area and research target unless a separate verified office exists.

Compliance Boundary

This research should be used to organize truthful public evidence. It should not be used to fabricate local photos, fake geotags, fake reviews, false service locations, insurance approval promises, engineering determinations, or guaranteed rankings.
